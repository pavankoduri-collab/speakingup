📊 Speaking Tips

Do not worry about mistakes ▼
Mistakes are how you learn. The goal is to communicate your idea clearly, not to be perfect. Every error is a chance to improve.
Slow down and breathe ▼
Many learners speak too fast when nervous. Take a breath, slow your pace, and you will sound more confident and be easier to understand.
Record yourself often ▼
Use your phone to record your speaking practice. Listen back and identify what went well and what to improve. This is the fastest way to track progress.
Use filler phrases naturally ▼
Instead of silence, use: "That is a great question", "Let me think about that", "What I mean is", "In other words". These give you thinking time and sound natural.

Could I take your name and date of birth? Caller: It is Sarah Mitchell, born on the 12th of March 1990. Receptionist: Thank you Sarah. Dr Chen has a slot next Tuesday at 2:15pm. Does that work? At the Airport
Travel English
Passenger: Excuse me, could you tell me where gate B12 is? Staff: Of course! Take the escalator to level two, follow signs for terminal B, and B12 is on your left after security. Passenger: Is that far? My flight boards in 45 minutes. Staff: You should be fine. Security is quick at this hour. Passenger: Is there a currency exchange nearby? Staff: Yes, right before the security checkpoint. They open at six in the morning.

🎧 How to Use Audio Practice

Step 1: Listen and understand ▼
Play the audio and focus on understanding the meaning. This trains natural comprehension.
Step 2: Listen and repeat (shadowing) ▼
Replay and repeat each sentence immediately after hearing it. Match the speed, rhythm, and intonation. This is called shadowing and is used by language learners worldwide.
Step 3: Record yourself ▼
Read the conversation aloud while recording yourself. Compare your recording to the original to identify differences in pronunciation.

🧠 Confidence-Building Techniques

🔁

Power Posing

Spend 2 minutes in an expansive posture before any speaking situation. This reduces stress and increases confidence hormones.

📔

Affirmation Journaling

Write 3 things you communicated well each day. Rewire your brain to notice success and build momentum.

🎭

Role-Play Scenarios

Practise difficult conversations beforehand. Rehearsing builds neural pathways so real situations feel familiar.

🌊

Breath and Pacing Control

Slow your breath before speaking. Speak at 70% of your normal speed. Pausing makes you seem thoughtful, not unsure.

🎯

Set Tiny Daily Goals

Make one small conversation each day: with a shop assistant, a colleague, or even a voice message. Consistency builds confidence.

📸

Record and Review Weekly

Record one 2-minute speaking exercise per week. Listen back after 4 weeks and you will be amazed at your progress.

Interview Preparation

Master the language and techniques for English job interviews.

The STAR Method

Use this framework for behavioural questions like "Tell me about a time when..."

S

Situation

Set the context. Describe where you were, your role, and the overall situation. Keep it to 1-2 sentences.

"In my previous role as a team lead, we were facing a critical deadline..."
T

Task

Explain your specific responsibility. What were YOU expected to do? Why was it challenging?

"My task was to coordinate 5 team members and deliver the project within 2 weeks..."
A

Action

Describe what YOU did. Use "I" not "we". Be specific about your actions and decisions.

"I immediately set up a shared project board and scheduled daily check-ins..."
R

Result

Share the outcome. Use numbers if possible. What did you learn? How did everyone benefit?

"We delivered 3 days ahead of schedule and the client renewed their contract."

💡 Always ask questions! It shows genuine interest and helps you assess if the role is right for you.
