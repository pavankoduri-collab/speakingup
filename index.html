📊 Speaking Tips for Every Level

Don't worry about mistakes ▼
Mistakes are how you learn. Native speakers make mistakes too. The goal is to communicate your idea clearly, not to be perfect. Every error is a chance to improve.
Slow down & breathe ▼
Many learners speak too fast when nervous. Take a breath, slow your pace, and you'll sound more confident and be easier to understand.
Record yourself often ▼
Use your phone to record your speaking practice. Listen back and identify what went well and what you want to improve. This is the fastest way to track progress.
Use filler phrases naturally ▼
Instead of silence, use: "That's a great question...", "Let me think about that for a second...", "What I mean is...", "In other words..." These give you thinking time and sound natural.

🎧 How to Use Audio Practice

Step 1: Listen & understand ▼
Play the audio and focus on understanding the meaning without reading. This trains natural comprehension.
Step 2: Listen & repeat (shadowing) ▼
Replay and repeat each sentence immediately after hearing it. Match the speed, rhythm, and intonation of the speaker. This is called "shadowing" and is used by polyglots worldwide.
Step 3: Read the transcript ▼
Check any words or phrases you missed. Look up unfamiliar vocabulary and add it to your word list.
Step 4: Record yourself ▼
Read the transcript aloud while recording yourself. Compare your recording to the original to identify differences in pronunciation and rhythm.

🧠 Confidence-Building Techniques

🔄

Power Posing Before Speaking

Spend 2 minutes in an expansive posture (hands on hips, chest open) before any speaking situation. Research shows this reduces cortisol and increases confidence hormones.

📔

Affirmation Journaling

Write down 3 things you communicated well each day. "I explained my idea clearly", "I used a new word correctly." Rewiring your brain to notice success.

🎭

Role-Play Scenarios

Practise difficult conversations beforehand. Rehearsing with a partner or mirror builds neural pathways so the real situation feels familiar and manageable.

🌊

Breath & Pacing Control

Slow your breath before speaking. Speak at 70% of your normal speed. Pausing is power — it makes you seem thoughtful, not unsure.

🎯

Set Tiny Daily Goals

Make one small conversation each day: with a shop assistant, a colleague, or even just leaving a voice message. Consistency builds confidence faster than big moments.

📱

Record & Review Weekly

Record one 2-minute speaking exercise per week. Listen back after 4 weeks — you'll be amazed at your progress. This is your most powerful confidence booster.

Interview Preparation

Master the language and techniques for English job interviews at any level.

The STAR Method

Use this framework to answer behavioural interview questions like "Tell me about a time when..."

⭐ S

Situation

Set the context. Describe where you were, what your role was, and the overall situation. Keep it brief — 1-2 sentences.

"In my previous role as a team lead at [Company], we were facing a critical deadline..."
⭐ T

Task

Explain your specific responsibility. What were YOU expected to do? Why was it challenging?

"My task was to coordinate 5 team members across 3 time zones and deliver the project within 2 weeks..."
⭐ A

Action

Describe exactly what YOU did. Use "I" not "we". Be specific about your actions, decisions, and skills.

"I immediately set up a shared project board, scheduled daily check-ins, and reassigned tasks based on each person's strengths..."
⭐ R

Result

Share the outcome. Use numbers if possible. What did you learn? How did the team benefit?

"We delivered 3 days ahead of schedule, the client renewed their contract, and I was promoted to Senior Lead."

💡 Always ask questions! Asking thoughtful questions shows interest, curiosity, and preparation. It also helps you assess if the role is right for you.
